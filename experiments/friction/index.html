Theory

Aim / Objective

To determine the coefficient of static friction (μₛ) between a wooden block and an inclined plane surface, using a pulley and hanging weight pan to apply a measured pulling force.

Block
Pulley

Theory

A wooden block of weight W rests on a plane inclined at a fixed angle θ. A cord attached to the block runs up the plane, over a smooth pulley at the top, and down to a hanging pan of weight W₁. Weight W resolves into W cosθ (normal to the plane) and W sinθ (along the plane, pulling the block down-slope). The hanging pan pulls the block up-slope with force W₁, opposed by friction.

As slotted weights are added to the pan, W₁ increases. The block remains at rest as long as static friction can supply the balancing force. Motion is impending the instant W₁ exactly balances W sinθ plus the maximum available friction force.

Central Formula

μₛ = (W₁ − W sinθ) / (W cosθ)

At the point of impending motion: W₁ = W sinθ + μₛ W cosθ, so μₛ = (W₁ − W sinθ) / (W cosθ). This is measured directly from weights and angle — no force gauges required.

Applications

Used to select gripper/conveyor materials, design safe ramps and belt drives, and estimate braking or anchoring forces on inclined surfaces in mechanical assemblies.

Limitations

Pulley bearing friction and rope elasticity add a small systematic error; readings can also vary a little between trials due to surface contamination, block alignment, and how gently the weight is added — this is normal, and is why several trials are averaged.

Precautions

Quick controls

Pick a Contact Surface and an Incline Angle in the left panel and bottom bar, then click or drag slotted weights onto the Block and into the Hanging Pan. Watch for "Impending Motion Reached" before you Record Reading.

Precautions

• Add weights to the pan gradually, not all at once.
• Keep the block's contact strip clean before each trial.
• Record the reading only after "Impending Motion Reached" is shown.
• Take all 5 trials for a reliable average μₛ.
• Do not change the angle once weights have been placed for a trial — reset first.
• Handle the pulley and pan gently; avoid jerking the rope.

Windows
